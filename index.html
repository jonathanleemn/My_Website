My Favorite Spots

San Juan de Gaztelugatxe

Bermeo, Basque Country

Located in the north of Spain; San Juan de Gaztelugatxe was absolutely breathtaking. I stayed in a villa and hiked 5 miles to visit the hermitage. Fun fact: It was also the filming site for the hit series Game of Thrones.

Italica

Seville, Andalusia

A once ancient Roman city and birthplace to Roman emperors. I couldn't believe my eyes how grand it was. It also served as the Dragon Pits in Game of Thrones; Fortunately, there were no dragons to be found here.

Seville Plaza Mayor

Seville, Andalusia

A beautiful spot found in the heart of Seville. At night, the plaza lights up and the fountain even changes color, creating an unforgettable ambiance. The plaza was bustling and felt very much alive.

Retiro Park

Madrid, Community of Madrid

A great place for picnics and walks. I remember stumbling upon a magic show in the middle of the park and unwittingly becoming a particpant in the antics. It's always a great time here.

Madrid

Madrid, Community of Madrid

Probably the most famous of Spain's cities and for good reason too. The city is teaming with energy and life, even at night! There's something for everyone here. Living an hour away by train, it was hard not to visit each weekend.

My school

Toledo, Castile-La Mancha

This is where I spent the majority of my waking hours. I taught 7th-12 grade and worked with an incredible and hardworking staff. María José even let me stay at her house for a few days when I first arrived to the village and didn't have a place to stay.

Antonio's home

Toledo, Castile-La Mancha

Spanish hospitality is the real deal. One of the teachers at my school, Antonio, would invite me over to his family's home for big Spanish lunches. I even got to go on excursions and attend festivals with his family, making me feel extremely welcomed in his home and community.

Real Alcázar

Seville, Andalusia

If you're ever in Seville, visiting the royal palace is a must! You can take a guided tour or explore the nooks and crannies of the palace and grounds on your own. It's no wonder Game of Thrones decided to film here.

Talavera de la Reina

Toledo, Castile-La Mancha

I decided to take a spontaneous trip to this town because it was really close to my village. I had no idea what to expect but ended up enjoying my day here. There aren't any big touristy attractions to be found here but one of the many great things about Spain is that even in small towns, there's still lots of beautiful sights to see.

Real Alcázar Gardens

Seville, Andalusia

I must've gotten stuck in the hedge maze for a good 30 minutes before finding my way out. Truly an unforgettable experience.

San Juan de Gaztelugatxe

Bermeo, Basque Country

There are 241 stone steps to reach the top of the hermitage. At the top of the crag, there's a large bell that supposedly grants wishes if you ring it three times and no more.

Real Alcázar

Seville, Andalusia

I'm gonna be honest; I had idea what purpose this "tomb-like" section served. I wasn't able to find any plaques around and there wasn't anyone else around. It it hadn't been for the area being well-lit I would've questioned if guests were even allowed in this area. Despite not knowing the history behind it, it was still a really cool spot to explore within the palace.
